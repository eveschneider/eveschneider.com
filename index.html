Evelyn Schneider

Geneva Board of Education Candidate

About Me

Geneva Board of Education Candidate Statement Evelyn Schneider Schneider4edu@gmail.com Occupation: Parent, School Counselor in a public school Education and Qualifications: BA, UIC; Teaching of Spanish MA, Concordia University, School Counselor 45 hours of additional coursework in various areas

As a parent of an 8th grader and a freshman in Geneva, I am passionate about public education. I began my education career in 1989 as a Spanish Teacher in public schools. In 1994 I was certified as a school counselor and have worked in this capacity since that time, again, in public schools.

I have had the fortunate experience of seeing students in a classroom setting as well as guiding them towards their future goals. I have witnessed all the pitfalls, crises, trials and tribulations that students go through in adolescence. For the past 10 years, I have also served as building leader where I work. In this role, I serve as a liaison between building administration and teachers. I also attend meetings with our district personnel.

My experience in education and by being involved in our school’s association, gives me a unique perspective on providing leadership, as well as complex decision-making. I come to you as a non-partisan leader who likes to bring the right people to the table in order to make the best decisions possible for students. I will facilitate decisive action for improvements. While future school funding remains uncertain, I will do the utmost to maintain a quality education for all students, high achieving and special needs, alike. While I would endeavor to improve graduation rates and test scores even more in Geneva, I do not want enrichment programs to fall by the wayside. I believe the Board’s job is to foster an educational environment where students thrive, not merely produce adequate test scores. My goals include:

  1. Quality of education; to create an environment where every child has the opportunity to succeed
  2. Equity of education for all students; technology awareness and resources for each student
  3. Active support for our teachers; keeping class sizes at a reasonable size, rich curriculum, diverse electives, professional development
  4. Accountability/transparency of administration at all levels
  5. District fiscal responsibility
  6. Continued support of parent participation in their child’s education

My experience has provided me with the knowledge, training and commitment necessary to lead Geneva during this critical period. I will continue to respect, value and encourage the involvement and contribution of Geneva’s parents, students and community.

For dedicated, passionate and experienced, leadership I ask you to vote for me. If elected, I will work hard, focus on pro-active change, and exercise leadership to foster consensus between teachers, administrators, parents, students, and the community at large.
